# Phishing Case Study

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers try to steal sensitive information like usernames, passwords, or bank details. They usually pretend to be a trusted source and trick people into sharing their information, similar to how bait is used to catch a fish. These attacks often come in the form of emails, messages, or fake websites that look very real.

In most cases, the attacker first sends a message that appears to come from a trusted source such as a bank or online service. The message often creates urgency, asking the user to act quickly. It usually includes a link that looks genuine, but actually leads to a fake website. Once the user opens the link and enters their details, the information is captured without their knowledge. The attacker can then use this data to access accounts or perform unauthorized actions.

For example, a college student receives an email that appears to be from their bank, asking them to update their account details urgently. The student clicks the link and enters their login details without verifying the source. Within a short time, the attacker gains access to the account and may misuse it or even transfer money.

Students and working professionals are common targets because they use online services frequently. The consequences of phishing can include financial loss, data theft, identity misuse, and loss of privacy. This shows how important it is to stay alert and verify any suspicious communication before taking action.
